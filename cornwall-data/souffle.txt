A soufflé is a baked egg-based dish originating in France in the early
eighteenth century. Combined with various other ingredients, it can be
served as a savory main dish or sweetened as a dessert. The word
soufflé is the past participle of the French verb souffler which means
"to blow," "to breathe," "to inflate," or "to puff." A soufflé is a
baked dish with a flavorful base mixed with beaten egg whites
