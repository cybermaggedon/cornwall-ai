Truro (/ˈtrʊəroʊ/; Cornish: Truru)[3] is a cathedral city and civil parish in
Cornwall, England. It is Cornwall's county town, sole city and centre for
administration, leisure and retail trading. Its population was 18,766 in the
2011 census.[1] People of Truro can be called Truronians. It grew as a trade
centre through its port and as a stannary town for tin mining. It became
mainland Britain's southernmost city in 1876, with the founding of the Diocese
of Truro. Sights include the Royal Cornwall Museum, Truro Cathedral (completed
1910), the Hall for Cornwall and Cornwall's Courts of Justice.",
